# Turbo Dismount (2014, Secret Exit) - Full Clone Catalog

Researched via the three fan wikis (dismount.fandom.com, turbodismountcentral.fandom.com, turbo-dismount.fandom.com, all fetched through the MediaWiki API since Fandom blocks plain fetches), the speedrun.com level database (matches in-game level order), the iOS App Store listing, Steam pages, and IGCD. Turbo Dismount launched 21 May 2014 (Steam Early Access started January 2014 with 10 vehicles / 11 levels per Wikipedia); the final game has 32 levels and 39 vehicles.

## Core mechanics (needed to make any level make sense)

- **Launch**: hold a button to charge a power bar that sets start speed (full bar = max). Tapping only = "Stalled". Taking zero damage = "Nailed It!". Damage awards points; the run ends into an instant replay (slow-mo down to 1/16x).
- **Hot Spots**: each level has fixed obstacle placement zones (rounded squares with yellow borders and arrows) where the player swaps in any obstacle from the catalog. Chicken is the only level with none.
- **Steering**: each level offers 3-6 preset routes (pick before launch); later levels add manual/free steering (introduced with Mall Chase).
- **Poses**: every vehicle has 3-5 dummy poses (in seat, on hood, hanging off the back, sitting on the wrecking ball, on the fire truck bumper...). Pose choice changes how the ragdoll takes damage.
- **Characters**: 8 ragdolls (Mr. Dismount, Mrs. Dismount, Mr. Ego, Mr. Heft, Mr. Stalwart, Mrs. Bumblebee, Mrs. Diva, Mr. Reach who has Slenderman proportions). Custom face photos and logos can be applied.
- **NPC traffic**: white "ghost" cars that noclip through each other and follow fixed tracks until the player touches one, which triggers a real cascading pileup. NPC roster: hatchback, smart car, medium car, minivan, pickup trucks, muscle car, bus, bulldozer, Hummer snowplow, white Maximus semi, police cars, monorail train.

## 1. All 32 levels (in-game order, per speedrun.com)

1. **Original Classic** - Short straight road with two Hot Spots pre-filled with a wall and a ramp; 12 trees, 2 billboards. Direct remake of the Truck Dismount (2003) default map and the level that auto-loads on first boot. Fun: the purest form of the game, charge, hit wall, ragdoll flies.
2. **Chicken** - Straight road with one oncoming NPC car on the other lane; 3 billboards, 20 palm trees, 60 barricades. The ONLY level with no obstacle slots, so vehicle and pose choice are everything. Fun: a pure game of head-on chicken.
3. **Aiming High** - Your road launches over/under an underpass with heavy cross traffic below; 52 "Low Blow" slabs ring the lower road, 10 trees, 3 billboards. Fun: aerial drop into moving traffic.
4. **Chicken Twist** - An S-curve road twisting right and left, 60 barricades lining it, 24 palm trees, varied oncoming traffic (mini car, car, bulldozer, Maximus semi, bus). Fun: Chicken but you must survive curves first.
5. **Space Program** - Long runway into an extremely tall, long ramp pointing at "space"; you fly up, then careen straight back into the ground. Only the fastest cars (Ledorean, Banditt, Sweet Justice, Squealer) reach the top. Achievement "To Space!": reach 1000 ft / 305 m. Fun: the height/airtime meme level.
6. **T-Junction** - Two roads meeting in a T; you drive from the stem into two lanes of fast cross traffic. 3 Hot Spots, 3 steering routes (default steers into a tree). One of the 3 demo levels. Achievement "Ping Pong": get hit by 5 different NPC vehicles in one run. Fun: the canonical traffic-pileup level.
7. **Skid Marks** - Drag strip versus NPC racers (mini car, minivan, car, pickup) with a stack of loose bricks at the finish. Obstacles placeable only on your lane; steering: stay in lane, swerve, or cut into the opponent's lane. Demo level; the community's favorite score/achievement farm (turbo pads + Wedge = ~100K points).
8. **Freeway** - An elevated freeway whose twin bridges have collapsed mid-span; you must jump the gap. Fast cars (Espionager, Banditt, Sweet Justice) clear it; slow/heavy ones (Maximus, Heavy Metal, Swinger) drop into the void.
9. **Storm Drain** - Start on a bridge, jump down into an LA-style concrete storm drain channel where a white Maximus semi passes frequently; default route is a head-on with the truck.
10. **Let's Keep Going** - A rocky cliff face with a 362 ft drop. One Hot Spot, 6 steering options: straight off, shallow left/right, sharp left/right off the cliff edge. Fun: Thelma & Louise simulator, long ragdoll falls.
11. **Scaramanga** - Two facing corkscrew/curved ramps (named after the Bond car barrel-roll jump in The Man with the Golden Gun); the goal is to barrel-roll across to the other side. Achievement "Scaramanga": successful barrel roll and landing. Fun: a precision stunt jump that almost always fails hilariously.
12. **Roundabout** - A circular road (roundabout) plus a ramp; fling yourself into circulating traffic or jump the middle.
13. **Loop De Loop** - A full vertical loop with 5 Hot Spots, trees around it. Most vehicles stall on the loop and drop; you need turbo pads plus a fast/light vehicle (the skateboard famously completes it). Part of the "Winding Ways" mobile pack. Fun: physics gauntlet.
14. **Like a Glove** - A parking lot full of parked (non-moving) vehicles with free steering; slalom or plow into the parked cars. (Ace Ventura reference.)
15. **Lane Merger** - Extremely fast two-lane traffic; you start on a curve that merges into the right lane. Merging cleanly is possible with perfect timing; everything else is a high-speed rear-ending. Fun: timing puzzle disguised as a crash level.
16. **Run DMZ** - An open field carpeted with land mines (pun on Run-DMC + demilitarized zone). Achievement "Border Incident": detonate 10 mines in one dismount (big vehicles like Maximus/Red Hot Engine make it easy). Fun: popcorn explosions and chained launches.
17. **Froggerer** - Many parallel roads of crossing traffic in front of you (Frogger homage) with orange fog. Crossing all lanes intact is near impossible. Community favorite for total-vehicle-demolition and somersault achievements.
18. **Merry-Go-Round** - A circular road with cars endlessly circling and land mines placed around the ring.
19. **Big Air Compo** - A downhill slope into a launch ramp, landing in a marked scoring field with painted lines and numbers 0-200; obstacles placeable on the ramp. Fun: ski-jump distance contest.
20. **Missing Delivery** - Colored cars driving endlessly around an infinity-loop track (reference to the puzzle game RGB Express); insert yourself into the loop.
21. **Mall Chase** - Mall parking lot with police cars parked at every corner; passing one triggers an instant ram-you pursuit. First level to introduce manual steering. If you are ejected, cops switch to running over your ragdoll. You can juke cops into trees. Fun: emergent chase chaos.
22. **Fast Lane** - Oval speedway clearly modeled on Indianapolis (big grandstand on the pit straight, tunnel opposite the start/finish line). Three racing lanes with NPC racers: slow hatchback inside, pickup mid, very fast muscle car outside; a turbo boost strip on the line; 2 Hot Spots. Fun: join a race that always becomes a wreck.
23. **Monorail Incident** - Several pathways leading toward a wall plus a broken elevated monorail bridge. On launch, a train exits the tunnel and derails off the broken rails onto the right pathway; time it right and the train crushes you. Slow vehicles (Heavy Metal) can't reach the train in time even with boost pads. Fun: scripted disaster set piece.
24. **Split Decision** - Three elevated roads carrying NPC buses, reachable via three ramps (short, medium, long); pick a ramp, fly into a bus broadside. Heavy Metal can only reach roads 1-2.
25. **Swiss Banks** - Alpine map: you start at the top; below are 2 roads and 1 rail line with a constantly passing train. Default route plunges straight down into the lower traffic/train. Heavy Metal with a boost can shove the train off its track.
26. **Hit the Fan** - Ramp jump into a wind farm of 12 spinning wind turbines, with barricades and "landmines galore" (the name is the pun). Fun: getting batted by giant rotating blades.
27. **Figure Scraping** - Christmas level: figure-8 / infinity-symbol ice track with the Hot Spots at the center crossing; NPC bulldozer and snowplow Hummer circulate; the Delivery Van carries 8 colored gift boxes here.
28. **The Getaway** - Large open map with sparse NPC traffic and police; on launch a police car breaks off and chases you; a huge brake pad waits in a parking lot. (Free bonus level on mobile.)
29. **The City Heights** - Elevated city highways enabling one very big jump; police zones around the map trigger chases. (Part of the "Law and Disorder" pack.)
30. **Highway Patrol** - A highway with a donut shop on the right and an overpass crossing it; many police cars (parked at the donut store and cruising); the default route runs past the shop and aggros all of them. ("Law and Disorder" pack.)
31. **Hornet's Nest** - You must run a gauntlet to reach a staircase to crash on, through stacked police chase radii, with lots of Hot Spots. Only the fastest vehicles (Banditt, Wedge, Sweet Justice, Quad-Fin Fighter, Squealer) make it. ("Law and Disorder" pack.)
32. **Roadblock Bridge** - Reach the end of a long bridge road past police posts that chase when you enter their zone; one billboard and a constantly running train. ("Law and Disorder" pack.)

Mobile level/vehicle DLC pack names (for theming reference): Law and Disorder, Speed & Style, Mighty Pedals, Speedy Results, The Unstoppables, Hard Times, Winding Ways, Total Torque, Impact Zone, Luxury Liners, plus a $5.99 Premium unlock.

## 2. All 39 vehicles

Caveat: fan-wiki speed figures are inconsistent (mixed mph/kph, contradictory "fastest" claims). Treat them as tier flavor: I tag each as Slow / Mid / Fast / Extreme. Useful design fact: unpowered vehicles (trike, skateboard, cart, chair) still respond to turbo/brake pads but show no exhaust flames; several vehicles have unique turbo-pad effects.

Original 19 (menu order):
1. **Delivery Van** - box van, no cab doors; the free starter vehicle. Mid speed, mid weight.
2. **The Wedge** - yellow open-top sports car (Ferrari F355 Spider / C3 Corvette mix). Extreme speed (wiki claims 300+ mph), notably unstable at top speed on preset routes. The go-to achievement car.
3. **Maximus** - cab-over semi truck (1966 White Freightliner). Slow, very heavy; also appears as white NPC traffic.
4. **Pink Lightning** - pink child's tricycle. Unpowered, very slow, indestructible model; the dummy takes all the damage. Achievement: complete 100 dismounts with it ("Timmy's Nightmare").
5. **Red Hot Engine** - fire truck whose ladder spins continuously while moving; one pose puts the dummy on the front bumper. Slow/heavy, indestructible sprite.
6. **Mall Racer** - shopping cart/trolley. Slow, unpowered; hard landings can slice the dummy in half on the handlebar; dummy can clip into the handlebars. 3 poses.
7. **Sasquatch** - monster truck with oversized wheels; despite the fantasy, it tends to flip over when trying to crush cars; best at climbing low obstacles. Slow-mid, heavy.
8. **Smooth Cruiser** - 4-door lowrider sedan (Homer Simpson pink-car vibes). Mid: ~110 mph, ~150 with boost.
9. **Corley** - Harley-style cruiser motorcycle (name nods to Harley-Davidson / Full Throttle's Corley Motors). Mid speed; the rider is exposed and launches off easily (Low Blows fling riders).
10. **Ranger** - WW2 military jeep (Ford GPW / Willys). Mid speed, sturdy.
11. **Street Slicer** - skateboard. Unpowered, very slow normally, indestructible; light enough to be the one vehicle that famously completes Loop De Loop.
12. **Komrade** - farm tractor (Zetor). Slow; at high speed it can slip past the Lane Blocker before the posts deploy.
13. **Cool Streak** - turquoise moped/scooter. Slow-mid; exposed rider.
14. **Boss Throne** - swivel office chair that spins as it rolls (a Jacksepticeye "like a boss" reference). Unpowered, slow, indestructible.
15. **Boogie Bus** - VW Type 2 camper van that constantly rocks back and forth, making every character look like they are dancing. Mid speed.
16. **Heavy Metal** - giant 8-wheel bulldozer, 4 exhausts. Slowest vehicle (~32 km/h) and densest: shovel plows through mines, brick walls and small barriers; barely reacts to mines; cannot jump ramps properly (shovel digs in); can push the train off the rails in Swiss Banks; a Road Bump can capsize it at speed; falls through a fan-made bridge from sheer mass. Indestructible sprite. "The Unstoppables" pack.
17. **The Swinger** - mobile wrecking-ball crane, the largest vehicle (~40 km/h). The ball swings on its chain into everything; one pose sits the dummy on the wrecking ball (Miley Cyrus "Wrecking Ball" joke).
18. **Token Bird** - bird-shaped flying kart that clucks like a chicken as it moves; one of only two flying vehicles; poor air handling.
19. **Shuriken** - orange sport bike (Suzuki GSX-R style; name riffs on Kawasaki Ninja). Fastest motorcycle: ~130-140 mph, 0-60 in ~2.4 s; understeers and falls over easily.

Seasonal/update vehicles (20 more):
20. **Grim Wagon** - funeral hearse; faster than other mid-class cars; rear door opens as a pose (ride in the coffin spot).
21. **Squealer** - Formula 1 car (Lotus T127, 2010). Extreme: fastest acceleration in the game, ~460 km/h top, minimal oversteer; came with a racing-helmet cosmetic.
22. **Wastelander** - dune buggy (Meyers Manx). Fast-mid: ~100 km/h with rapid acceleration, average handling.
23. **Ricky-Tick** - 1930s Ford Model T hot rod with exposed supercharged engine. Fast-mid.
24. **Ledorean** - DeLorean-style "futuristic" car that leaves glowing tire trails (Back to the Future); one of only four cars fast enough to top Space Program.
25. **Tumbler** - quad bike / ATV; exposed rider, flips readily.
26. **Royal Highness** - London double-decker bus; constantly shuffles left-right, so quick turns tip it over. Slow/heavy, tall (blocked by the Overpass obstacle).
27. **Quad-Fin Fighter** - X-Wing-style fighter jet. Fastest vehicle overall (~500 mph, ~780 boosted). Signature gimmick: hitting a turbo pad locks its wings into an X and it fires 4 laser blasts that knock over brick walls, bowling pins, and NPC cars (only while undamaged). Tons of detachable parts. Free on mobile.
28. **Banditt** - 1967 Shelby Mustang GT500 muscle car. Extreme: billed as the fastest car (~250 mph, ~360 boosted).
29. **Sweet Justice** - police car (same model as NPC cops). Fast.
30. **Espionager** - Aston Martin DB6 spy coupe (Finn McMissile vibes). Fast (~270 km/h) with the best handling.
31. **Pivot** - a motorized sofa with pillows. Slow, silly, dummy unrestrained.
32. **Magnum** - 1930s Bentley 4.5 Litre Blower-style cabriolet; slow, V16-like sound, spare wheel on the side, and no windshield or roof, so the driver is ejected in fast corners.
33. **Jugger** - tank. Slow even among big vehicles, indestructible sprite; turbo pads make its turret fire at NPC vehicles and objects (one of two armed vehicles, with the Quad-Fin).
34. **Loco** - a road-going steam locomotive built on the Delivery Van chassis; blows a train whistle on turbo pads.
35. **Old Creaky** - mining handcar pulling a few carts; its walking beam squeaks faster as it speeds up. Wooden, slow.
36. **Kantele** - wooden "instrument car" (the kantele is a Finnish zither); plays a string/guitar sound when it hits a turbo pad. Faster than it looks.
37. **Whiplash** - fairground bumper car; very bouncy (gets punted across the map when hit hard); its contact pole sparks instead of showing exhaust flames on turbo pads. Indestructible sprite.
38. **Jamboree** - beach buggy (Meyers Manx) with a surfboard mounted on top (the board is a loose decoration).
39. **Lance** - Toyota HiAce-style ambulance with a movable stretcher inside; rear-door stretcher pose. Mid speed (~85).

Useful taxonomy for a clone: 3 motorcycles (Corley, Shuriken, Cool Streak), 2 flyers (Token Bird, Quad-Fin Fighter), 2 armed (Jugger, Quad-Fin), 3 emergency (Red Hot Engine, Lance, Sweet Justice), 8 with indestructible models (Pink Lightning, Street Slicer, Mall Racer, Boss Throne, Red Hot Engine, Jugger, Heavy Metal, Whiplash), 4 unpowered "joke" rides (trike, skateboard, cart, office chair), heavies (Maximus, Heavy Metal, Swinger, Royal Highness, Jugger), extremes (Wedge, Banditt, Squealer, Quad-Fin, Espionager, Ledorean).

## 3. Most iconic levels (best 5 for a clone)

Strongest evidence-backed picks:
1. **Original Classic** - the franchise's identity (Truck Dismount homage), the default level on first boot, and one of the 3 demo levels. Cheapest to build: road + wall + ramp.
2. **T-Junction** - demo level, has its own achievement ("Ping Pong"), and is the only TD1 level Secret Exit brought back by name as an official level in Turbo Dismount 2, the clearest signal of icon status. Teaches the traffic-pileup core loop.
3. **Space Program** - the meme level (giant ramp "to space", 1000 ft achievement); every Let's Play (Markiplier, Jacksepticeye, who made the game blow up in 2014-2015) gravitates to it. Maximum airtime, minimal geometry.
4. **Skid Marks** - third demo level; the community's standard farming level for score and achievement runs (turbo pads down the drag lane, brick stack finish, rival racers to swerve into).
5. **Mall Chase** - introduced manual steering and the police-chase mechanic that the whole back half of the game (Law and Disorder pack) was built on; cops ramming you and chasing your ejected ragdoll is emergent-comedy gold.

Strong alternates if you want variety: **Loop De Loop** (the visual icon, a physics challenge: most vehicles can't finish the loop), **Run DMZ** (minefield, pure explosion chaos, very cheap to implement), **Fast Lane** (Indy-style speedway; TD2's official "Daytona Speedway" level confirms the oval-racing concept is core), **Chicken** (simplest possible level, zero obstacles, pure head-on).

## 4. Props/obstacles catalog (placeable in Hot Spots)

Pads:
- **Turbo Pad** - boosts speed on contact; triggers per-vehicle gimmicks (Quad-Fin lasers, Jugger cannon shot, Loco whistle, Kantele music, Whiplash sparks); exhaust flame effect on powered vehicles.
- **Brake Pad** - slows the vehicle, can bring it to a full stop.
- **Oilslick** - oil puddle that severely impairs traction/handling.

Ramps (a graded family):
- **Big Ramp** - huge ramp curving up to ~90 degrees; vertical launch at speed.
- **Semi-Big Ramp** / **Medium Ramp** / **Quarter Pipe** / **Mini Ramp** - the same idea in decreasing sizes (Quarter Pipe is white with a yellow arrow, slightly bigger than Mini Ramp).
- **Twist Left / Twist Right** - corkscrew ramps that roll the vehicle mid-air (barrel rolls).
- **Ramps** (paired/retracting) - angled ~30 degree ramps that send the vehicle rolling randomly left/right.

Walls and blockers:
- **Brick Wall** - built from 108 individual physics bricks; shatters spectacularly and feeds the score (Quad-Fin lasers can also dislodge bricks).
- **Mega Wall** - indestructible monolith big enough to block most straight approaches; the full-stop crash prop.
- **Low Blow** - low indestructible stone slab; stops ground-level vehicles dead and flings exposed riders over the handlebars; tall-wheeled vehicles (Sasquatch) can climb it.
- **Lane Blocker** - three posts that pop up out of the road as the vehicle approaches; indestructible (the Komrade can outrun the deploy animation at speed).
- **Overpass** - a railing-less bridge piece; tall vehicles (bus, semi) slam into it, short ones pass under.
- **Roadblock** - white/blue barrier, physics-enabled, heavier to shove aside than cones.

Hazards and toys:
- **Land Mines / Minefield** - explode on contact; cannot damage vehicles directly but launch them off course (stronger versus light vehicles) and DO damage the dummy directly.
- **Speed Bumps / Road Bump** - bumpy squares that make fast vehicles jump; can capsize even Heavy Metal at speed.
- **Traffic Cones** - light scatter props, gravity-affected, damage on ram but trivial to plow through.
- **Bowling Pins** - giant pins ("bigger traffic cones"); knockdowns for points; can be toppled by the Quad-Fin's lasers.
- **Loot Crate** - physical box that opens when approached and releases entities that impair vehicle handling (late mobile addition).

Level-native (non-placeable) hazards worth cloning: NPC traffic streams, chasing police cars with aggro radii, derailing monorail train (Monorail Incident / Swiss Banks / Roadblock Bridge), spinning wind turbines (Hit the Fan), collapsed bridge gaps (Freeway), cliff drops (Let's Keep Going), destructible trees and billboards (trees have a "cut down 100 trees" achievement).

## Bonus: Turbo Dismount 2 (Early Access Jan 2025, 1.0 March 2026)

- Official levels (8, per the fan database turbodismount2.com, reliability moderate): Super-Speed (huge floating 4-lane highway), Going Down (vertical multi-floor cascade), Dismount Derby (demolition arena), Mine Skydive Classic (mineshaft drop + skydive), Daytona Speedway (banked oval), Expressway (multi-lane freeway), T-Junction (returning classic), Wobbles (bumpy stability-breaking terrain), plus Steam Workshop levels.

## Data caveats

- Fan wiki speed/handling numbers mix units and contradict each other (Wedge vs Banditt vs Squealer each called "fastest"); use the tier labels, not the raw numbers.
- The 32-level list and order comes from speedrun.com's level database and is corroborated by the wikis (the main fandom wiki's navbox is missing 6 of them: Big Air Compo, Hit the Fan, Figure Scraping, The Getaway, Hornet's Nest, Roadblock Bridge, which I sourced from the other two wikis and the App Store bundle listing).
- turbodismount2.com is an SEO-style fan site; its TD2 vehicle stat blocks (8 vehicles with x/10 ratings) conflict with IGCD's much longer identified list, so trust IGCD/Steam for TD2 vehicles.

# Turbo Dismount (Secret Exit, 2014) - Core Gameplay Loop and Mechanics Research

Physics-based "vehicular impact simulator," official sequel to Stair Dismount (2002) and reimagining of Truck Dismount (2003). Tagline: "A kinetic tragedy about Mr. Dismount and the cars who love him." Unity engine. Steam Early Access Jan 10, 2014; full release May 21, 2014 ($9.99, Windows/macOS); iOS Sep 24, 2014 and Android Nov 12, 2014 (free + IAP, $5.99 premium unlock). Steam version: everything unlocked from the start; mobile gates vehicles/levels behind IAP packs ($0.99 bundles like "Speed & Style", "Hard Times", "Luxury Liners", "Impact Zone", "Total Torque").

## 1. One round, step by step

1. **Main menu** (Steam): Play, Customizations, Options/Exit, Follow Secret Exit, Technical Support. Mobile adds Achievements and Leaderboards buttons. Customizations = upload a photo for the dummy's face (256x256 or 512x512) and a custom vehicle logo decal.
2. **Map select**: grid of level thumbnails. "The Original Classic" (a remake of the Truck Dismount map: short road, a wall and a ramp) loads by default on first boot. 32 maps total in the final version (12 free on mobile, rest premium). Steam Workshop levels can also be downloaded and played.
3. **Setup screen**: the level loads with the vehicle sitting at its start point. Six setup buttons (plus Dismount and Back): **Characters, Vehicle, Obstacles, Poses, Steering, Camera**.
   - **Characters** (8): Mr. Dismount (default, medium durability), Ms. Dismount (slimmer, slightly lower durability), Mr. Heft (fat, durable), Ms. Diva (fat female, durable), Mr. Reach (very long thin limbs, Slenderman-like, low durability), Mr. Stalwart (stocky, durable, bad balance), Ms. Bumblebee (large hips, durable hips), Mr. Ego (huge head, durable head, bad balance). Characters differ in body shape, durability, and balance, which genuinely changes crash outcomes. All ragdolls break into **14 body pieces**.
   - **Vehicle**: choose one of ~35 vehicles (launch had 10). Each has distinct mass/speed/durability. Full list with traits below.
   - **Poses**: cycle the dummy through 3 to 5 vehicle-specific poses (see section 3).
   - **Obstacles**: place props into predefined "Hot Spots" (see section 4).
   - **Steering**: pick one of several **predefined steering paths** per map, shown as a line of white dots on the road; the vehicle auto-follows the chosen path. A later update added optional **manual steering** (arrow keys / gamepad) during the run.
   - **Camera**: pre-select perspective: Character, Vehicle, First Person, or Cinematic.
4. **Launch ("Dismount")**: press and **hold Space** (hold the Dismount bar on mobile). A power/speed gauge starts empty, then fills and depletes in an oscillating cycle. **Release at the moment that sets launch velocity**; a full bar = max velocity. Gamezebo: even a small speed difference "can mean the difference between a 6-digit and a 7-digit high score." Timing also matters versus moving NPC traffic. Just tapping Space leaves the car motionless; after a 3-second timer you get the **"Stalled"** message ("Press and hold to dismount").
5. **The crash plays out** with no further input (unless manually steering). Damage icons and points pop up per event, a running score tally counts up, a combo multiplier builds on rapid consecutive impacts.
6. **End of run**: "Dismount Complete" banner (or **"Nailed It!"** if you took zero damage: no broken bones, no lost vehicle parts; "Nailed It" 100 times = an achievement). A 3-2-1 countdown, then the game drops into the **instant replay**.
7. **Replay/score screen**: review the crash (section 5), see total score, personal best, and per-level online leaderboard rank. Then **Reset** to set up the next dismount (resetting erases the stored replay). Restarting is near-instant; the loop is seconds long by design.

## 2. Scoring system

**Principle: damage awards points.** Two damage streams: pain inflicted on the dummy (primary) and structural damage to vehicles (player's and NPC vehicles, supplementary). Per-level high scores feed Steam Leaderboards / Game Center.

**What scores:**
- **Dummy impacts**: points scale with impact force per body part. The body is 14 pieces; each fracture, dislocation, or amputation is a discrete scoring event with its own popup icon.
- **Injury catalogue** (from the wiki "Damage Type" page and NamuWiki): bone break, shoulder break/dislocation, "instant" (high-force) bone break, skull fracture (includes neck), inner-skull break, rib fracture, spine fracture, pelvic/hip fracture, limb amputation, head decapitation, torso cut in half. Fractured/severed ankles, shins, thighs, wrists, elbows, shoulders show generic bone icons; skull/rib/spine/pelvis and cuts have dedicated icons.
- **Vehicle break** (parts shearing off your vehicle) and **other vehicle break** (NPC vehicles you damage). Vehicle destruction ranges from cosmetic dents to full disassembly and scales the score. Multi-vehicle chain reactions (your hit causes NPC-on-NPC collisions) are big.
- **Acrobatics**: somersault/flip count and **spin/airborne count** are tracked and scored (achievement: 10 somersaults in one dismount). Airtime/altitude pays ("flying limit" event for very high launches); head-first landings pay extra.
- **Explosives**: mine detonations launch and score heavily, "often pushing scores into the millions."
- **Destroying obstacles/scenery**: brick walls, bowling pins, traffic cones, trees (tracked: "cut down 100 trees" achievement).
- **Sustained contact**: continuous grinding ("sandwiching" the dummy between two cars, vehicle pinning him against a wall) keeps generating impact ticks. WriterParty: "The longer the sandwiching happens, the more points you rack up."

**Multiplier/combo**: each hit "triggers a multiplier, which increases the points for further sustained injuries." Quick consecutive impacts raise it; players report sustaining a **10x multiplier** during continuous-contact glitches, so the visible combo multiplier goes at least to 10x. Keeping the dummy in constant collision keeps the multiplier alive.

**Display during the crash**: a running score tally on the HUD counts up live (Pocket Gamer: "a running tally of injuries incurred by Mr Dismount"); each damage event spawns a floating popup at the impact point with the injury icon plus points; multiplier shown alongside. No gore, just icons and crunchy sound effects (a deliberate design decision by Secret Exit).

**Magnitudes**: ordinary decent runs are 6 to 7 digits; a legitimate strong score cited is ~21 million on Freeway; leaderboard cheaters sit at 250 million. So a faithful clone should produce scores in the hundreds of thousands to tens of millions.

**No star ratings, no score targets, no unlock currency** in this game. Progression pressure comes only from per-level leaderboards (Steam/Game Center), personal bests, and 23 achievements. (Grokipedia claims "three-star ratings" but no other source supports that for TD1; treat it as wrong. The sequel TD2 added medals/time trials.) Notable achievements that double as mechanics documentation: To Space! (reach 1000 ft / 305 m height in Space Program), 88mph! (cross Skid Marks finish at 88 mph; a speedometer/finish-line speed readout exists there), Look at Him Go! (record time on Skid Marks, so that level has a timer), Dizzy Yet? (10 somersaults in one run), Border Incident (detonate 10 mines in one run on Run DMZ), Ping Pong (hit by 5 different NPC vehicles in T-Junction), Just a Flesh Wound (lose exactly one limb), Disintegration (fully obliterate the dummy), Bluesmobile (totally demolish a vehicle), French Revolution (1000 lifetime decapitations), Speed is Key (3 consecutive max-velocity launches), The Dude Abides (10 strikes with Bowling Pins).

## 3. Character posing system

- Every vehicle has **3 to 5 poses** per character; the Poses button cycles them. Poses are presented as silhouette/thumbnail icons, not officially named in-game (no published canonical name list exists).
- Mr. and Ms. Dismount share identical pose sets per vehicle; other characters can have different sets (NamuWiki notes pose sets differ between characters on some vehicles, e.g. only Mr. Dismount gets the Wastelander's Mad-Max "blood bag" pose strapped to the front of the car; the Jamboree surf-wagon has a pose quirk involving Mr. Heft/Mr. Reach).
- Typical pose archetypes seen across vehicles: seated normally in/on the vehicle; lying flat on the hood or roof (superman-style); standing on the roof or seat (surfer); clinging to the front bumper/grille; sitting backwards or on the rear; riding the fire truck's ladder; riding The Swinger's wrecking ball (a Miley Cyrus "Wrecking Ball" parody pose); motorcycle variants like standing on the saddle.
- **Poses matter mechanically**: they change the dummy's initial stability, center of mass, launch angle and trajectory, and which body parts hit first (a front-bumper pose pressing weight on the front wheel stops motorcycles from wheelie-ing, per NamuWiki strategy notes).

## 4. Obstacle / prop placement

- Placement is **slot-based, not free-form**: each map contains a number of **Hot Spots**, rendered as rounded squares with yellow corner borders and arrows through the middle. The Obstacles menu assigns one prop (or Empty) per hot spot. Every map has at least one hot spot except "Chicken," which has none. A newer patch lets you nudge/move a placed device while holding **Shift**. Free placement only exists in the separate Steam Workshop **level editor** (Update #6), where creators also define NPC vehicle routes.
- **Prop list** (cross-referenced wiki + NamuWiki): Empty Square (clears slot); Big Ramp (vertical launcher, used for loop setups); Semi-Big Ramp; Medium Ramp; Mini Ramp (low elevation); Quarter Pipe (distance launcher vs Big Ramp's height); Twist Left / Twist Right ramps (jump ramps that rotate the car left/right mid-air); Ramps (twisting ramp that rolls the vehicle); Road Bump (speed bump, big recoil at high speed); Brick Wall (destructible individual bricks, scoring debris); Mega Wall (indestructible blocker); Low Blow (low indestructible parking-style wheel stop, launches motorcycles, jumpable); Overpass (height-limit bar that stops tall vehicles); Lane Blocker (rising barricade, indestructible); Roadblock (sturdy road sign); Traffic Cones (weak, knock-over debris); Bowling Pins (large knockable pins, strikes tracked); Oil Slick (kills traction, induces spins); Turbo Pad (speed boost, exhaust flame; special effects on some vehicles: Quad-Fin Fighter fires 12 lasers, Jugger fires 3 cannon shots, Sweet Justice sirens, LeDorean leaves fire trails, Kantele plays electric guitar); Brake Pad (slows or stops the vehicle); Land Mine (explodes on contact, hurls vehicle and dummy); Minefield (cluster of mines); Loot Crate (opens and fires dozens of golden bolts that shred vehicles).
- Props can help or hurt; the design intent is mixing them with vehicle, pose, speed, and steering path for experimentation.

## 5. Camera, slow motion, replay

- **Pre-launch/live camera modes**: Character (follows dummy), Vehicle (chase cam), First Person, Cinematic. Plus a **free-fly camera**: WASD to move, Q/E down/up, Shift faster, Ctrl slower, mouse-drag to orbit, scroll wheel (or Z/X) to zoom.
- **Instant replay** after every completed dismount (auto-entered after the 3-2-1 countdown). A **speed slider in the bottom left** scrubs playback through discrete steps: 4x, 2x, 1x, 1/2x, 1/4x, 1/8x, 1/16x, 0x (freeze), and the same steps **in reverse** to rewind. Watch from any angle with the free camera, including first person. Pressing Reset discards the replay and returns to setup. Mobile had Everyplay video export (GIF/video sharing with fast forward, rewind, slow motion baked into one clip).
- During the live crash the game does NOT force slow motion; slow-mo is a replay feature (achievement "Let's See That Again!" = view a replay in slow motion).

## 6. Desktop controls

- **Space**: hold to charge the dismount power gauge, release to launch; also confirms Reset.
- **Mouse drag**: orbit camera; **scroll wheel** (or Z/X): zoom.
- **Free-fly cam**: W/A/S/D forward/left/back/right, Q/E down/up, Shift speed up, Ctrl slow down.
- **Arrow keys**: manual steering during a run (post-update feature); gamepad supported (added later; remappable via launcher key/button map, labels reportedly poor).
- **Shift + drag**: move an obstacle within its hot spot (later patches).
- Mobile equivalents: touch-hold the Dismount bar, drag to orbit, pinch zoom, on-screen steering wheel option.

- **Vehicles** (~35 final; 10 at launch), with traits: Delivery Van (medium all-round), The Wedge (fastest fragile sports wedge), Maximus (Peterbilt 320 semi, slow, crushing mass), Pink Lightning (kid's trike, slowest, indestructible), Red Hot Engine (fire truck, durable, slow, ride-able ladder), Mall Racer (shopping cart, indestructible), Sasquatch (monster truck, fast and strong), Smooth Cruiser (convertible), Corley (Harley Fat Boy, wheelies), Ranger (Willys jeep, durable), Street Slicer (skateboard, unstable), Komrade (Zetor 25 tractor), Cool Streak (Vespa scooter), Boss Throne (office swivel chair, extremely slow, spins), Boogie Bus (minibus that idles with a shake), Heavy Metal (snowplow, strongest pusher), The Swinger (crane with indestructible wrecking ball), Token Bird (toy bird, slips), Shuriken (Hayabusa superbike, fastest straight line, wheelie-prone), Grim Wagon (hearse limo, bottoms out on ramps), Squealer (race car, best handling, low floor), Wastelander (Mad Max car), Ricky-Tick (classic car with detachable decorative engine), LeDorean (BTTF DeLorean parody, fire trails on boost), Tumbler (worst-handling motorcycle), Quad-Fin Fighter (X-Wing parody, boost pads fire 12 destructive lasers, accelerates mid-air), Royal Highness (double-decker bus, top deck shears off), Banditt (1970 Mustang, balanced fast), Sweet Justice (police car, fragile front), Espionager (Aston DB6 spy car, rear booster panel), Pivot (sofa, terrible tracking), Magnum (long classic car), Jugger (tank, fires on boost), Loco (locomotive, can't climb ramps), Old Creaky (3-car tram, rear cars are dead weight), Kantele (Finnish-instrument car, wooden tires, brown skid marks), Whiplash (bumper car, bounces hard), Jamboree (surf wagon), Lance (ambulance with rattling rear doors and a stretcher pose that can slide out). Vehicles right themselves onto their wheels when flipped (roly-poly behavior). Non-engine vehicles still respond to turbo/brake pads but show no exhaust boost effect. Some vehicles are visually indestructible (Pink Lightning, Red Hot Engine, Mall Racer, Street Slicer, Boss Throne, Jugger, Whiplash) but still emit damage effects.
- **NPC vehicles** on routes: small car (slow), medium car, truck, bus (very durable), minibus, bulldozer, snowplow (most dangerous), monorail/NPC train (rail-bound, unaffected by Low Blow/Mega Wall until derailed), and the **police car**, the only hostile AI: it sirens and actively chases/steers toward the player, useful for extra collision points.
- **Maps** (32): The Original Classic, Chicken, T-Junction, Storm Drain, Let's Keep Going, Scaramanga, Big Air Compo, Missing Delivery, Mall Chase, Hit the Fan, Figure Scraping, The Getaway, Chicken Twist, Lane Merger, Loop De Loop, Roundabout, Freeway, Skid Marks (race/timed, finish line with speed readout), Space Program (vertical height level with altitude readout in ft/m), Merry-Go-Round, Aiming High, Frogerer, Like a Glove (static parking lot, no moving NPCs), Run DMZ (minefield level), Fast Lane, Monorail Incident, Swiss Bank, Split Decision, The City Heights, Highway Patrol, Hornet's Nest, Roadblock Bridge.
- 23 Steam achievements, Steam Workshop level sharing, trading cards, per-level leaderboards. Metacritic 85 (iOS). ~482k Steam sales, 50M+ Google Play installs.
- Tone/design: no blood or gore; emotional punch comes from crunchy impact audio; dummy is "an abstraction of a human figure" (Jani Kahrama).

**Confidence notes**: scoring formula internals (exact points per joint, multiplier decay timing) were never published; everything above about scoring composition is sourced from the developer-adjacent descriptions, wiki damage-type pages, and player reports. Pose names are not documented anywhere; pose archetypes above come from the Wastelander/Swinger/Jamboree documentation plus widely recorded gameplay footage, so name them freely in a clone.
